1. Executive Summary
TransitOps is a centralized, role-based transport operations platform that digitizes the full lifecycle of fleet management
for logistics companies — from vehicle registration and driver compliance to trip dispatch, maintenance, fuel/expense
tracking, and financial analytics.
Most SMB logistics operators today run this entire workflow on spreadsheets and paper logbooks (or on generic tools like
Ecount), leading to double-booked vehicles, missed maintenance, expired driver licenses going unnoticed, and no real
visibility into per-vehicle profitability. TransitOps replaces that with one login-gated system where every action —
dispatching a trip, logging fuel, closing a maintenance ticket — automatically updates vehicle/driver status and rolls up
into cost and performance analytics.
This document defines the product scope, functional requirements, business rules, and MVP prioritization for an 8-hour
build.
2. Problem Statement
Transport and logistics SMBs typically manage fleet operations through disconnected tools: WhatsApp for dispatch, Excel
for fuel logs, paper registers for maintenance, and manual reminders (or none) for license/document expiry. This causes:
Vehicles or drivers double-booked onto overlapping trips
Suspended or license-expired drivers still being assigned
Overloaded vehicles (cargo exceeding rated capacity)
Missed maintenance leading to breakdowns
No visibility into which vehicle is actually profitable (fuel + maintenance cost vs revenue)
No early warning when a driver/vehicle’s fuel efficiency degrades — often the first sign of engine wear, tyre
problems, or fuel pilferage
3. Product Vision & Differentiation
3.1 Vision
A single system where every stakeholder — Fleet Manager, Dispatcher, Safety Officer, Financial Analyst — works off the
same live data, and the system itself enforces the business rules that today rely on someone remembering them.

6. Functional Requirements
6.1 Authentication & RBAC
Email + password login; session via JWT (access + refresh token)
Role selected/assigned at account level, not selectable at login (mock login screen shows role selector for
demo/testing convenience only)
Only authenticated users can access any application route
Failed login lockout after 5 attempts (per wireframe)
Each role is scoped to specific navigation items and API permissions (see RBAC matrix in Design Doc §5)
6.2 Dashboard
KPI tiles: Active Vehicles, Available Vehicles, Vehicles in Maintenance, Active Trips, Pending Trips, Drivers on Duty,
Fleet Utilization %
Filters: Vehicle Type, Status, Region
Recent Trips table (trip id, vehicle, driver, status, ETA)
Vehicle Status distribution (Available / On Trip / In Shop / Retired)
Reminders panel (enhancement): upcoming license/document expiries, mileage anomaly flags, vehicles overdue for
depot return
6.3 Vehicle Registry
Fields: Registration Number (unique, validated), Name/Model, Type (Van/Truck/Mini/etc.), Max Load Capacity (kg),
Odometer, Acquisition Cost, Status
Status values: Available, On Trip, In Shop, Retired
Enhancement — Vehicle Document sub-panel: RC Book, Insurance, PUC, Permit, each with number + expiry date;
feeds the dashboard reminder panel
Enhancement — vehicle profile shows lifetime category-wise expense totals (Fuel / Maintenance / Tolls / Misc) and
computed ROI
6.4 Driver Management
Fields: Name, License Number, License Category, License Expiry Date, Contact Number, Safety Score, Status
Status values: Available, On Trip, Off Duty, Suspended
Expired license or Suspended status automatically excludes driver from trip assignment pool
Safety Officer role can adjust Safety Score and Suspended status; other roles view read-only
6.5 Trip Management
Create trip: Source, Destination, Vehicle (available only), Driver (available only), Cargo Weight, Planned Distance
Enhancement fields: Load Type (e.g., Palletized/Loose/Container), Freight Type (what is being carried — e.g.,
FMCG, Industrial, Perishable), Declared Cargo Capacity used (%)
Trip lifecycle: Draft → Dispatched → Completed → Cancelled
Live board shows trips grouped by lifecycle stage
On Completion: capture final odometer + fuel consumed → auto-creates a fuel log entry tied to that trip
Enhancement — Trip chaining: system tracks the sequence of trips per vehicle; if a vehicle has not returned to
its home depot within a configurable number of trips/days, it is flagged on the dashboard reminder panel
6.6 Maintenance
Create maintenance record: Vehicle, Service Type, Cost, Date, Status (In Shop / Completed)
Creating an active maintenance record automatically sets vehicle status to In Shop and removes it from dispatch

7. Business Rules (Mandatory + Derived)
1. Vehicle registration number must be unique.
2. Retired or In Shop vehicles never appear in the dispatch/vehicle-selection pool.
3. Drivers with expired licenses or Suspended status cannot be assigned to trips.
4. A driver or vehicle already marked On Trip cannot be assigned to another trip.
5. Cargo Weight must not exceed the vehicle’s maximum load capacity — dispatch is blocked otherwise, with a clear
validation message (as shown in wireframe).
6. Dispatching a trip automatically sets both vehicle and driver status to On Trip.
7. Completing a trip automatically sets both vehicle and driver status back to Available, and creates the associated
fuel log entry.
8. Cancelling a dispatched trip restores vehicle and driver to Available.
9. Creating an active maintenance record automatically sets vehicle status to In Shop.

10. Closing maintenance restores vehicle to Available, unless the vehicle is Retired.
11. (Enhancement) A fuel log entry more than [threshold]% below the vehicle’s rolling average mileage triggers a
Mileage Deviation Alert.
12. (Enhancement) A vehicle not returned to its designated home depot within [N] trips or [N] days triggers a Depot

13. (Enhancement) A document (RC/Insurance/PUC/Permit) within [N] days of expiry triggers a reminder on the
dashboard; an expired document blocks the vehicle from dispatch (configurable — off by default for MVP demo to
avoid blocking test data).
14. (Stretch) A tyre that reaches its position-specific wear threshold before the fleet-average km is flagged for early-
replacement review.
8. Non-Functional Requirements
Responsive web UI (desktop-first, usable on tablet)
API response time target: < 500ms for standard CRUD/list endpoints
Role-based authorization enforced at the API layer, not just hidden in UI
Data integrity: all status transitions happen via transactional service-layer calls, never direct field edits, to prevent
inconsistent states
Auditable: key transitions (dispatch, complete, cancel, maintenance open/close) timestamped and attributable to
the acting user
9. Data Entities (Overview)
Users, Roles, Vehicles, Vehicle Documents, Drivers, Trips, Trip Expenses (Fuel Logs), Other Expenses, Maintenance
Records, Tyres, Tyre Position History, Notifications/Reminders.
Full schema with fields, keys, and relationships is defined in the TransitOps Design Document, Section 3.
10. Acceptance Criteria (Sample — Core Flow)
Given the example workflow in the problem statement:
1. Register vehicle ‘Van-05’ (capacity 500kg, status Available) → appears in Vehicle Registry, unique reg. no. enforced.
2. Register driver ‘Alex’ with valid license → appears in Available drivers pool.
3. Create trip, Cargo Weight 450kg → system validates 450 ≤ 500 → dispatch allowed.
4. Dispatch → Vehicle & Driver status become On Trip; both disappear from other trips’ selection pools.
5. Complete trip (final odometer + fuel consumed) → Vehicle & Driver return to Available; fuel log auto-created;
mileage computed.
6. Create maintenance record (Oil Change) → Vehicle status becomes In Shop, hidden from dispatch.
7. Close maintenance → Vehicle returns to Available.
8. Reports reflect updated operational cost and fuel efficiency immediately.
Each step above must be independently testable and demoable.

12. Risks & Mitigations
Risk Mitigation
8-hour window too tight for full stack + all enhancements Strict MoSCoW cut line; Must-list is independently demoable end-
to-end
Real vehicle-document lookup APIs require paid access/KYC Stub with a mock lookup service returning sample data,
documented as a roadmap item
Tyre 2D UI is complex for remaining time Ship as simple position-select dropdown + history table if drag-
drop isn’t feasible
Business-rule bugs (e.g., race condition on double-dispatch) Enforce all transitions via atomic DB transactions in the service
layer, not client-side checks alone
